# University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC)

The **University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign** (UIUC) is a public land-grant research university in Illinois in the twin cities of Champaign and Urbana. It is the flagship institution of the University of Illinois system and was founded in 1867.

## Key Facts
- **Location**: Urbana and Champaign, Illinois, USA.
- **Motto**: "Learning and Labor".
- **Reputation**: UIUC is considered a "Public Ivy" and is globally renowned for its engineering, computer science, and library and information science programs.

## Academics & Research
UIUC is a powerhouse in research and education.
- **Grainger College of Engineering**: Consistently ranked among the top engineering schools in the world.
- **Computer Science**: Famous for its contributions to the development of the modern internet (e.g., Mosaic web browser), PLATO system, and LLVM.
- **Gies College of Business**: A highly ranked business school.

## Campus Life
The university has a massive library system, one of the largest among public universities in the US. It also has a vibrant Greek life and numerous student organizations. The "Marching Illini" is a famous collegiate marching band.
